30% of people experience chronic loneliness, and 80% report feeling lonely in different situations from childhood through old age.

Our sense of connectedness with others has a profound effect on physical and mental health and well-being. Loneliness—or perceived social isolation—accelerates aging and increases cardiovascular health risks, blood pressure, and even predicted mortality rates. Those who are more lonely often experience more depressive symptoms, exhibit impaired cognitive performance and decline, and have a higher risk of Alzheimer’s and dementia. Loneliness can cause you to feel hostile, stressed, pessimistic, anxious, and have low self-esteem.

Loneliness is the social equivalent of physical pain, hunger, and thirst and causes you to feel unsafe.

This triggers your fight-or-flight response and defers all control from your Conscious, thinking mind to your Subconscious, survival mind. That is why, even though you decide to meet new people, go outside your comfort zone, or connect more with the people around you, your subconscious programming can override your conscious decisions through your thought patterns, hidden fears, and perceptions. That is what keeps you stuck in the cycle of loneliness.
